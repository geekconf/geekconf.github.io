µService

A master workshop on microservice ownership, engineering, tools, and organizational impact.

September, 2015

Sessions

Mastering the design of microservices means understanding high-level distributed systems concepts, software performance dimensions, data dimensionality, and clear interface contracts. Mastering the development and ownership of microservices means understanding common deployment architectures, implementing intelligent monitoring, managing overhead, and understanding your clients.

The µService workshop includes ten 45 minute sessions covering this material. A 15 minute Q&A and break is provided between each session. The sessions are spread evenly over the first two days of the workshop.

Opening Remarks
An introduction to the presenter, tutors, subject, and schedule.
Monoliths and Microservices
Define each and discuss the pros and cons of each approach. Draw parallels between SOA and other system design paradigms.
Discovery, Synchrony, and Coupling
Coupling service names to runtime addresses, and service requests to responses. Microserviecs are often praised for loose coupling; what does that mean? Why is coupling bad?
Asynchrony and Uncertainty
Decoupling requests from resonses can help trim latency, buffer customers from availability issues, and smooth write bursts. But using asynchrony effectively requires an in-depth appreciation for distributed state issues.
Interface Contract is Everything
A poorly planned API usually has catestrophic consequenses. What goes into an API? How do you design interfaces that provide real encapsulation, survive scaling exercises, and inform your customers?
Deployment Architectures
A survey of SOA deployment architectures.
What is Ownership?
A distributed code base is built quickly in parallel and maintained by a distributed workforce of owners. What trade-offs should an organization make to empower service owners without sacrificing quality and while aligning the interests of all parties.
Monitor All the Things!
Performance profiling and localized fault detection is natural in a microservices environment. Lets talk about time series data and valuable application metrics.
Overhead and Repetition
Each microservice deployed incurs a similar degree of overhead in resources like network, hardware, monitoring, alarming, budgeting, and support. Squash what you can with automation.
A Culture of Cooperation
Clear interface contracts can take you far, but eventually you will need partners to prioritize changes or migrations. This session is about issues that can and often do come up in a microservice oriented organization.

Instructor

Sessions are presented by Jeff Nickoloff, founding member of All in Geek Consulting Services.

He owned and engineered over 300 microservices with Amazon.com. A mix of platform and product services, they had a blended internal/external customer base. Jeff oversaw the deprecation of multiple decade old services, and the migration of hundreds of internal clients.
