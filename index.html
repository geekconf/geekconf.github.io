Sessions

Learn it

So you want to become a microservices master?

You and your organization can implement a microservices approach, but expertise hinges on a complete command of Design, Development, and Ownership.

The µService Workshop’s purpose is to give attendees total comprehension in these three areas. Design incorporates an understanding of high-level distributed systems concepts, software performance dimensions, data dimensionality, and clear interface contracts. Development and Ownership encompasses common deployment architectures, implementing intelligent monitoring, managing overhead, and truly understanding your clients.

Over the course of the first two days of the µService Workshop, attendees will take part in ten 45 minute learning sessions paired with 15 minutes of Q&A. Each session is designed to provide a thorough investigation of different aspects of microservice Design, Development, and Ownership.

Getting Started
Get to know your µService instructor and tutors. We’ll take a quick look at the subject matter, as well as the schedule and structure of the workshop.
Monoliths and Microservices
What’s the difference between a monolithic and a microservices approach? Is one better than the other? We’ll define each, take a look at pros and cons, and draw parallels between SOA and other system design paradigms.
Discovery, Synchrony, and Coupling
Coupling service names to runtime addresses, and service requests to responses. Microservices are often praised for loose coupling, but what exactly does that mean? And why is coupling bad?
Asynchrony and Uncertainty
Decoupling requests from responses can help trim latency, buffer customers from availability issues, and smooth write bursts. We’ll spend some time developing an in-depth appreciation for distributed state issues in order to use asynchrony effectively.
Interface Contract is Everything
What goes into the planning of an API? Is it possible to design interfaces that provide real encapsulation, survive scaling exercises, and simultaneously inform your customers? Get ahead of the API game now and avoid catastrophic consequences later.
Deployment Architectures
What does it actually take to build and run an SOA? We’ll overview various deployment architectures.
What is Ownership?
A distributed code base can be built quickly and maintained by a distributed workforce of owners. But what trade-offs should an organization make to empower service owners without sacrificing quality, all while aligning the interests of individual parties?
Monitor All the Things!
In a microservices environment, performance profiling and localized fault detection are natural. We’ll talk about time series data and valuable application metrics.
Overhead and Repetition
Each microservice deployed will incur a similar degree of overhead in resources - network, hardware, monitoring, alarming, budgeting, and support. But what can you squash with automation?
A Culture of Cooperation
Clear interface contracts can take you far, but eventually you will need partners to prioritize changes or migrations. What issues can be anticipated in a microservice-oriented organization?

Project

Practice it

Ready to use what you’ve learned?

Because of the social nature of microservices, the µService workshop culminates in a group project where participants work together to break a monolithic application into microservices. Through their work together, groups will learn how to manage the technical and social complexities of microservice adoption. Together, we will accomplish the following:

  1. Deconstruction of a monolithic service
  2. Forming teams and designing interfaces
  3. Negotiating and sharing interfaces
  4. Implementation of each with a different technology stack
  5. Deployment and integration
  6. Exercise #1: Support
  7. Exercise #2: Volume
  8. Exercise #3: Traffic Shape
  9. Exercise #4: Latency
  10. Exercise #5: Deprecation and Migration
